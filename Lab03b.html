Nairobi - Kenya

Culture

Nairobi, Kenya is a city of rich culture. A large portion of Nairobi culture consists of their diverse music such as Benga and Rhumba, dance music derived from different styles of hip-hop. Nairobi is also a place of many religions including Christianity, Hindism and Islam. One of the more notable festivals celebrated is Jamhuri Day, a day celebrating Kenya's independence.

Climate

The climate in Nairobi consists of short warm summers and cool dry winters. On average the daily high during the summer in Nairobi is 26 degrees C with a daily low of 16 degrees C. During the winter the high is around 23 degrees C with a low of 13 degrees C. Overall the temperature in Nairobi stays relatively consistent regardless of the time of year.

Cuisine

Nairobi cuisine is a mix of traditional Kenyan dishes, international influence and a variety of street food. One of the more popular dishes is Ugali and Nyama Choma. This dish consists of a porridge base known as Ugali and a side of grilled meat (most commonly being chicken, beef or goat). A variety of dishes are also served as street food inlcuding samosas, roasted maize, and bhajias.

History

Nairobi was founded during the British colonial era as a settlement during the construction of a railway line in the area. It became the main location for keeping military supplies and eventually gained popularity due to its location and steady rise in merchants, settlers and government officials. In the 1950's Nairobi became a central city for anti-colonial activism and played a significant role in the Mau Mau Uprising (rebellion against british rule).
